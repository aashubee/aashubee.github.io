Project Overview

This PCB Design project was focussed on the ideation, cretaion, and assembly of a MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface) controller, with added funtionality for playback and 'chopping.' As part of IEEE's HOPE (PCB Design) class, me and my team completed the entire board, from the layout to the soldering of components to creat the final product. Our board would take multiple inputs, including button presses, a potentimeer, and a slider, translating them into musical notes that can be seen with a DAW (Digital Audio Workstation). We also added additional functionality (apart from MIDI workflow) by adding a microphone and a speaker, able to capture audio and playback on the controller. This

Input / MIDI Mode

• 12 buttons map to note-on/note-off messages over USB MIDI, each routed to a DAW.
• A potentiometer and a knob send continuous controller changes (e.g., pan, reverb, filter) as MIDI CC messages.
• All inputs are debounced and translated into MIDI text messages for the host computer.

Record / Playback Mode

• Button 1: start/stop recording from the mic (record LED indicator).
• Button 2: play back the recorded clip (play LED indicator).
• Mode switching keeps MIDI controls separate from audio capture to avoid conflicts.
• From there, rest of button functionality used to chop, playback, reverse etc audio clip, for vocal chopping effects.

Design Approach

There were a few constarints we had in mind when desinging. Primairly, burdget and x/y space. We know we wanted at least 12 input buttons (coresponding to 12 muscial notes) along with some adjustment scale such as a slider. Because the limiting factor for space was the x/y that our switch array took up, pacakaging all components was not too big of a challenge. Instead, our challenge came in ensuring that we would have good signal integrity in all our lines, esepcially power and the parth from the microphone to the speaker (which uses an I2S communication protocol). We decided to use an ESP32, to be able to easily program our IC to both preform USB data outputs (for MIDI mode), and also the basic programming to manipulate recorded audio with the buttons and slider (Mic mode).
